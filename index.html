Endangered Mammals

From majestic tigers to gentle giants, these mammals face critical threats to their survival in the wild.

Bengal Tiger

The Bengal tiger is the most numerous tiger subspecies, with populations primarily in India, Bangladesh, Nepal, and Bhutan.

Asian Elephant

Population decline caused by deforestation and illegal ivory trade.

Mountain Gorilla

Powerful yet gentle primates found in the volcanic mountains of Central Africa.

Orangutan

Highly intelligent primates known for their reddish-brown hair and tree-dwelling habits.

Giant Panda

Known for their distinctive black and white coloring, giant pandas are native to China and primarily eat bamboo.

Raccoon

Raccoons are known for their intelligence and adaptability, but they face threats from habitat loss and hunting.

Endangered Birds

These remarkable birds face habitat loss and environmental challenges that threaten their existence.

Southern Cassowary

Large flightless bird crucial for rainforest seed dispersal in Australia and New Guinea.

Lilac-crowned Parrot

Beautiful parrot endemic to Mexico's Pacific slope, facing severe population decline.

Emperor Penguin

Antarctica's largest penguin species, vulnerable to climate change impacts.

Hyacinth Macaw

World's largest flying parrot species with stunning cobalt-blue plumage.

Whooping Crane

North America's tallest bird, saved from near extinction through intensive conservation.

Philippine Eagle

One of the world's largest and rarest eagles, found only in the Philippines.

Endangered Reptiles

Ancient survivors now facing modern threats‚Äîthese reptiles need our protection to thrive.

Hawksbill Sea Turtle

Critically endangered marine turtle known for its beautiful shell and important role in coral reef ecosystems.

Komodo Dragon

The largest living lizard, found in Indonesia, threatened by habitat loss and poaching.

Green Sea Turtle

Endangered marine turtle known for its olive-brown shell and herbivorous diet.

Gharial

Critically endangered crocodilian with a long, thin snout, found in the rivers of India and Nepal.

Loggerhead Sea Turtle

Endangered marine turtle known for its large head and strong jaws, found in oceans worldwide.

Saltwater Crocodile

The largest living reptile, found in coastal areas of Southeast Asia and Australia, threatened by habitat loss and hunting.

Endangered Amphibians

Sensitive indicators of environmental health, these amphibians are among the most threatened species on Earth.

Green Tree Frog

A common amphibian found in trees, known for its vibrant green color and distinctive call.

Poison Dart Frog

Brightly colored amphibians known for their toxic skin, found in Central and South America.

Fire Salamander

A striking black and yellow amphibian found in European forests, threatened by habitat loss and pollution.

Golden Mantella

A small, brightly colored frog native to Madagascar, threatened by habitat loss.

Cane Toad

A large, terrestrial toad native to Central and South America, known for its adaptability and invasive nature.

Tiger Salamander

A large, black and yellow spotted salamander found in North America, threatened by habitat loss and pollution.
